Filipino-style Spaghetti

Dish Description

Spaghetti is a dish that is well known across the world. This opens it up for a diverse range of styles across different culinary cultures. This spaghetti dish that will be represented here is the Filipino-style Spaghetti.

Ingredients (For 8 servings):

  • 1 pound uncooked spaghetti
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 4 Filipino-style hotdogs
  • 1 onion; peeled and chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic; peeled and minced
  • 1 bell pepper; seeded and chopped
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 2 cups tomato sauce
  • 1/2 cup tomato paste
  • 1 cup banana ketchup; preferably sweet and spicy
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1/2 cup quick-melt cheese; shredded

Instructions:

  1. Boil spaghetti in salted water on medium heat according to the pack's instructions or until firm to bite. Drain well, reserving some pasta water.
  2. Heat oil in a large saucepan. Add the hotdogs into the saucepan and cook them whilst occasionally stirring every now and then for about 1 to 2 minutes. Remove and set aside once lightly browned.
  3. Add onions, garlic and bell peppers to the pan and cook until soft.
  4. Cook ground beef in the pan, occasionally stirring whilst also breaking beef into smaller pieces, until it is lightly browned. Drain excess fat.
  5. Add the tomato sauce, tomato paste, banana ketchup, beef broth and some sugar.
  6. Boil and then lower heat to simmer whilst covered, for about 1 hour or until the meat is fully cooked and the sauce is thickened. If sauce is too thick for your liking, add a half cup of water incrementally until desired thickness is achieved.
  7. In the final 10 minutes of cooking, add the hotdogs, and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve with spaghetti sauce on the pasta topped with shredded cheese.
